1. The Function: re.sub()
The sub stands for substitute. It follows this logic:
re.sub(pattern, replacement, original_text)

Pattern: What to look for.

Replacement: What to put in its place (in your case, "", which is an empty string/nothing).

Original Text: The variable clean_command.

2. The Pattern: r"(search|from|on|wikipedia|...)"
r: Stands for "raw string." It tells Python to treat backslashes literally (standard practice for regex).

(): A grouping container.

|: This is the OR operator. It tells the code to look for "search" OR "from" OR "on," and so on.

3. What it actually does
If your clean_command was originally:

"search wikipedia for Albert Einstein"

The code looks for any of the words in your list and replaces them with nothing. The result stored back into clean_command would be:

" for Albert Einstein"
